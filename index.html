English Grammar: WH-Questions

"Nothing is particularly hard if you divide it into small jobs." - Henry Ford

WH-Question Words Tutorial

What?

Usage: Asking for information about something or asking for repetition/confirmation

Examples:

  • What is your name?
  • What? I can't hear you.
  • What do you want?
  • In what way did I approach you?
What...for?

Usage: Asking for a reason (why)

Examples:

  • What did you do that for?
  • What are we going to use this machine for?
  • For what reason did they deport him?
When?

Usage: Asking about time

Examples:

  • When did you leave?
  • When will you visit us?
  • When is your birthday?
Where?

Usage: Asking about position/location

Examples:

  • Where do you live?
  • Where is Bartın located?
  • Where will you we get our bus tickets?
Which?

Usage: Asking about choice, for identification

Examples:

  • Which movie do you want to watch?
  • Can you tell me which of these pens is yours?
  • Which colour do you prefer?
  • In which country is the Çanakkale bridge found?
Who?

Usage: Asking which person/people (Subject)

Examples:

  • Who opened the door?
  • Who will take you to work tomorrow?
  • Who is your role model?
  • Who did this to you?
Whom?

Usage: Asking which person/people (Object)

Examples:

  • Whom did you see?
  • To whom did you give the envelope?
  • With whom are you planning to travel?
  • Whom did you consult before making the decision?
Whose?

Usage: Asking about ownership

Examples:

  • Whose keys are these?
  • Whose turn is it?
  • In whose bag did you put your phone?
  • From whose room did take that hair dryer?
Why?

Usage: Asking for reason

Examples:

  • Why did you call me?
  • Why are we using this route?
  • Why would she choose you instead of me?
Why don't?

Usage: Making a suggestion

Examples:

  • Why don't you watch English series to improve your English?
  • Why don't you go to the doctor for a check-up?
  • If you believe that you should have passed, why don't you go and consult your teacher?
How?

Usage: Asking about condition, quality and manner

Examples:

  • How are you feeling today?
  • How does this machine function?
  • How did the students perform in the examination?
How + adjective/adverb?

Usage: Asking about extent or degree

Examples:

  • How good is John in Mathematics?
  • How fast is the BMW X5?
How far?

Usage: Asking distance or progress

Examples:

  • How far is İstanbul from Ankara?
  • How far have you progressed on your project?
  • How far did you walk today?
How long?

Usage: Asking length or time length

Examples:

  • How long will this conference take?
  • How long is this railway line?
  • How long is one day?
  • How long is the İnkumu Beach?
How many?

Usage: Asking quantity (countable)

Examples:

  • How many ice creams should I buy?
  • For how many decades did you live in Turkey?
  • How many years did it take you to speak Turkish so fluently?
How much?

Usage: Asking quantity (uncountable)

Examples:

  • How much money do you have?
  • How much experience is required for me to get employed in this company?
  • How much water should a person drink per day?
How old?

Usage: Asking age

Examples:

  • How old are you?
  • How old will he be 10 years from now?
  • How old were we when we took this picture?
How come? (Informal)

Usage: Asking for a reason (why) - informal

Examples:

  • How come she has not arrived yet?
  • Samuel had said he will be here by 14:00 but how come I can't see him?
